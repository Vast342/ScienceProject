NO2

Introduction

Nitrogen dioxide (NO2) is a highly reactive gas, commonly used to represent the whole of the NOx gases. NO2 is primarily created by the burning of fuel and fossil fuels. Cars, planes, power plants, and buses are just some of the sources of Nitrogen dioxide. However, nitrogen dioxide is not a useful gas. In fact, it is extremely harmful to people, animals, and the environment.

Human Effects

If inhaled by humans, NO2 irritates the lungs and can trigger asthma, causing difficulty breathing. In large quantities, it increases risk of asthma and susceptibility to respiratory infection. NO2 can react with other chemicals in the air and create both particulate matter and ozone, both of which are harmful.

Environmental Effects

Nitrogen dioxide can interact with water and chemicals in the atmosphere to form acid rain, harming numerous ecosystems. The aforementioned nitrate particles cause haze, smog, and nutrient pollution in waters.

Solutions

However, the amount of nitrogen dioxide produced can be reduced by reducing electricity use and using cars less. A few examples include the use of solar panels to reduce the strain on the electrical grid, and using lower power usage lights such as LEDs.
